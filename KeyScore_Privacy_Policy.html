Privacy Policy for Key Score

Last Updated: March 5, 2026

Introduction

Key Score is a piano flashcard learning application developed by Austin Brown. This Privacy Policy explains how information is handled when you use the Key Score mobile application.

Key Score is designed to respect user privacy. The app does not collect, transmit, or share personal data. All data generated by the app is stored locally on your device and remains under your control.

If you have questions about this policy, you may contact:

Austin Brown

Email: abrown@elementengineering.org

Information We Collect

Key Score does not collect personal information and does not require users to create accounts or provide identifying information.

The app stores limited gameplay-related information locally on your device to provide features such as statistics and settings. This information never leaves your device.

Locally Stored Data

The following information may be stored on your device using Apple’s local data storage framework (SwiftData):

App Settings

  • Countdown timer settings (enabled/disabled and duration)
  • Accidentals enabled or disabled
  • Sensitivity settings based on selected instrument preset
  • Visual color highlight preferences
  • Difficulty level
  • Predefined MIDI range endpoints (reserved for future features)

Practice Sessions

  • Session start and end timestamps
  • Active practice time (excluding paused time)
  • Correct note count during the session

Note Events

  • Timestamp when a target note is displayed
  • Target MIDI note value
  • Time required to correctly play the note (measured in milliseconds)

These data points are used solely to compute statistics displayed within the app, including:

  • Total practice time
  • Recent activity (such as the last 7 days)
  • Average time to play notes correctly
  • Personal high scores

All of this information remains stored only on the device.

Microphone Access and Audio Processing

Key Score uses the device microphone during gameplay to detect musical notes played on a piano.

Important details about microphone use:

  • The microphone is used only during gameplay.
  • Audio is processed in real time for pitch detection.
  • No audio recordings are saved.
  • No raw microphone data is stored.
  • No audio is transmitted off the device.
  • The audio session is deactivated when gameplay is paused or exited, which releases the microphone.
  • The app requests microphone permission through Apple’s standard iOS permission system before gameplay begins.

Microphone access is required for the app’s core functionality.

Information We Do Not Collect

Key Score does not collect or store any of the following:

  • Personal identifiers
  • Names or email addresses
  • User accounts or login credentials
  • Audio recordings
  • Device identifiers
  • Advertising identifiers (IDFA)
  • Location data
  • Contact lists
  • Photos or files

Analytics, Advertising, and Third-Party Services

Key Score does not integrate any third-party SDKs or services.

Specifically:

  • No analytics tools are used
  • No advertising networks are used
  • No third-party tracking technologies are used
  • No cloud services are used by the app

The app does not transmit data to external servers.

Note that Apple may independently collect certain diagnostic information when apps are distributed through the App Store. Any such data is managed by Apple and is subject to Apple’s own privacy policies.

Data Storage and Security

All data generated by Key Score is stored locally on the user’s device using Apple’s SwiftData storage framework.

Because the app does not transmit or store data on external servers:

  • No user data is stored remotely
  • No information is shared with the developer or third parties
  • Data remains under the user’s control on their device

If the app is deleted, the locally stored data may also be removed according to the device’s operating system behavior.

Children’s Privacy

Key Score is an educational music practice tool intended for general audiences and may be used by people of all ages.

The app does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 or from any users. Because the app does not collect personal data, it does not maintain databases of user information.

Parents or guardians who have concerns about privacy may contact the developer using the email address listed below.

User Rights

Because Key Score does not collect or store personal information on external servers:

  • There is no personal data held by the developer
  • Users do not need to request access, correction, or deletion of personal information

Users can control their stored app data directly on their device by modifying settings or deleting the application.

If a future update introduces features that collect, transmit, or store data outside your device, this policy will be updated prior to those features being released, and users will be notified through the app or App Store update notes.

When updates are made, the “Last Updated” date at the top of this policy will be revised.
